The Challenge

Challenges of the Murids

Many students in North Macedonia live on limited monthly budgets and are managing tuition, rent, food, documentation, and travel expenses away from home. Although the desire for Deedar is strong, the practical cost of airfare, accommodation in Berlin, meals, local transport, and visa-related preparation places the journey beyond the reach of several students.

With departure ideally around 1 July and the Deedar scheduled for 4 July, time is short. Early clarity and support can help students prepare documents, arrange travel, and move with dignity and peace of mind.

The Aim

Aim for the Murids

The purpose of this initiative is to uphold the spirit of the Jamat: that no murid should feel alone when a sacred opportunity is within reach but financial hardship stands in the way. Supporting these students is not merely covering a ticket or a bed; it is helping young members of the Jamat participate in a deeply meaningful spiritual occasion.

In this spirit, the proposed support focuses on essential travel needs only: airfare, accommodation, basic food, and necessary journey-related expenses. The intention is simple, transparent, and rooted in collective care.
