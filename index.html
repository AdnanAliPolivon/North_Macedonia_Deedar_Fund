The Challenge

One month, high costs, and students who cannot move forward without support.

Several Ismaili students from Pakistan and Tajikistan are currently studying bachelor’s and master’s programs at South East European University in North Macedonia. They are willing to travel to Berlin for Deedar, but many have not been able to arrange their travel documents because the core barrier is financial: airfare, accommodation, and short-notice travel costs.

With Deedar on 4 July 2026 and the practical departure target around 1 July 2026, the support decision needs to happen quickly so students can prepare documents, book accommodation, and move with confidence.

Aim of the Murids

To make the journey possible for students whose intention is sincere but whose resources are limited.

01

Spiritual access

Support students in reaching Berlin for a once-in-a-generation spiritual gathering and Deedar opportunity.

02

Student dignity

Help students without making the process feel like a public burden or personal embarrassment.

03

Practical readiness

Cover the two major barriers: airfare and accommodation, with flexible ranges that can be updated as prices change.
