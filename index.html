A humble appeal to the Jamat

Helping students share in a sacred moment

Many of these young murids have never had the opportunity to attend a Deedari before. The forthcoming Deedar in Berlin is not simply a journey to another city; for them it is a deeply spiritual moment of belonging, hope, and connection with the Imam-of-the-Time.

As students living away from home, their intention is strong, but their means are limited. In the spirit of One Jamat, this page respectfully presents their estimated travel and stay needs to blessed members of the Jamat who may wish to help make this sacred opportunity possible with dignity and care.

This website is informational only. It does not collect payments or process donations. It is prepared to help coordinators, donors, and well-wishers understand the estimated need, student count, and range of support required.

The Challenge

Challenges of the Murids

The students are currently studying at South East European University in North Macedonia. Most are managing tuition, rent, food, residence matters, documentation, and daily expenses while living far from home. Their wish for Deedar is sincere, but the combined cost of airfare, accommodation, basic sustenance, and travel preparation is beyond what many can manage alone.

With departure expected around 1 July and Deedar on 5 July, there is very little time left. Early support can help them arrange documents, confirm travel, and prepare for this sacred occasion with peace of mind rather than anxiety.

The Aim

Aim for the Murids

The aim is to uphold the ethic of the Jamat: that when a murid is spiritually eager but financially constrained, the wider Jamat can become a source of mercy, support, and practical care.

The proposed assistance focuses only on essential needs: airfare, modest shared accommodation, basic sustenance, and journey-related necessities. The intention is not luxury; it is to help students reach Berlin respectfully, safely, and with dignity.

A final note

This website is only an informational proposal. It does not collect money, process transactions, or act as a payment channel. Its purpose is to present the students' estimated need clearly so that any support can be discussed and coordinated through the appropriate trusted channels.

For those blessed members of the Jamat who are able to assist, even partial support can carry great meaning. Helping a student attend Deedar is not only helping with travel expenses; it is helping a young murid experience a sacred moment they may remember for the rest of their life.
